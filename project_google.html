Public Insecurities (2014)

This project is one of a series that deals with insecurities pulled from Google’s search “suggestions” - the search completions that drop down from the search bar as you type. These suggestions come from private searches, recorded by Google, which are anonymized and re-presented to us. The ones I am interested in are expressions of collective, private insecurities that can be uncovered by typing a few, charged words into a search bar.

Public Insecurities is a single channel video projection that was installed in the 900 N Michigan Ave Bloomingdale's in Chicago from Aug 15th, 2014 to Sept 25th, 2014. The below video was played on a loop in a secluded space on the top floor of the mall.

The project is part of an ongoing research path.
